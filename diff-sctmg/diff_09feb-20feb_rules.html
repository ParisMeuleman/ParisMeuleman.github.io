V.02 - update 20.02.2026 1.0 CORE CONCEPTS This section of the core rules contains the essential framework that is used in all games of StarCraft: The Miniatures Game. 1.1 MODELS The Archon Studio miniatures that make up your army are models. Each model is any single entity (be it a Marine, a Zergling or a massive Ultralisk) mounted on its own independent base. While the model itself represents the character or vehicle, the following rules strictly govern the model via its base. 1.2 UNITS An army is composed of Units. A Unit consists of one or more models that act together as a single cohesive formation. Composition: The number of models in a Unit is defined by its Unit Card. For example, a basic infantry Unit might consist of several soldiers, while a heavy siege vehicle might operate as a Unit of one. Cohesion: Models within a Unit fight, move, and die collectively. They are bound by the rules of Coherency (section 1.6.4), representing the discipline and communication required to operate on a battlefield. Friendly &amp; Enemy: Units in your army are referred to as friendly Units. Likewise, Units in your opponent’s army are referred to as enemy Units.

1.5 THE TOOLS OF WAR To play games of StarCraft: The Miniatures Game, you will need some six-sided dice and a range ruler or tape measure marked in inches (“). 1.5.1 DICE The game uses standard six-sided dice, referred to as D6. D6: If a rule requires a D6, roll one die. The result is the number shown on the face. D3: If a rule requires a D3, roll one D6 and halve the result, rounding up: Roll 1–2 = 1 Roll 3–4 = 2 Roll 5–6 = 3 Multiple Dice (2D6, 3D6): If a rule requires, for example, 2D6, roll two dice and add the results together. 1.5.2 ROLL-OFFS During play, there will be rules that instruct the players to ‘Roll-off’ (such as determining who sets up terrain or deciding who the First Player is) to do so: Both players roll 2D6. The player with the higher total wins. If the totals are equal, roll again until a winner is determined. 1.5.3 RE-ROLLS Some rules may have instructions for mandatory or optional re-rolls. To re-roll a dice, use the same dice used for the original roll again. The same dice cannot be re-rolled more than once. 1.5.4 MODIFIERS In StarCraft: The Miniatures Game, Modifiers change the Target Number of a dice roll. When a rule grants a Modifier (written as ‘+X’ or ‘-X’), apply it before rolling the dice: ‘+X’ Modifier: This makes the roll easier. You reduce the Target Number of the roll by X. ‘-X’ Modifier: This makes the roll harder. You increase the Target Number of the roll by X. EXAMPLE A Marine needs a 5+ to hit a target: If he gains a +1 Modifier. The Target Number is reduced by 1. He now hits on a 4+. He suffers a -1 Modifier. The Target Number is increased by 1. He now hits on a 6+. Numerical modifiers to Dice Rolls (e.g., +1 to Hit) from different named sources (e.g., two different Special Abilities) are cumulative unless stated otherwise.

1.5.5 FIXED ADDITIONS VS. MODIFIERS If a rule says "D3+1", this is not a modifier to a Target Number. It instructs you to roll the die and add 1 to the final result (e.g. generating 4 damage instead of 3). 1.5.6 AUTOMATIC SUCCESS AND FAILURE Regardless of how many modifiers apply: An unmodified roll of 6 is always a Success. An unmodified roll of 1 is always a Failure. Exception: If a Unit has a Null Value (-) Characteristic, no roll for this characteristic is allowed. 1.5.7 CHARACTERISTIC AND ATTRIBUTE TESTS Whenever a rule instructs a player to make a Roll, it falls into one of two categories based on the source of the number being tested: Characteristic Test: A test that uses a value found on the Unit’s Characteristic Profile (e.g. checking Armour or Evade). Attribute Test: A test that uses a value printed on a Weapon Profile or within a Special Rule (e.g. checking a Weapon's Hit value). Value Generation: Rolls made to determine a distance or quantity (such as a Charge Roll or D3 Damage) are not Tests. These are resolved by adding the die result to the base value (see 1.5.5 Fixed Additions). TEST RESOLUTION SEQUENCE To resolve either type of test: Identify the stated value (the base Target Number). Apply any Modifiers (see above) to determine the final Target Number. Roll the required die. Success: The roll result is equal to or higher than the final Target Number. Failure: The roll result is lower than the final Target Number. 1.5.8 COCKED DICE If a die lands at an angle (cocked) or falls off the table, the roll is void. Re-roll that die immediately. Players should agree before the game begins on what constitutes a "cocked" die to avoid disputes during critical moments.

COHERENCY LINK When a rule tells you to move or place a Unit, the controlling player moves or places the Leading Model first, then places the remaining models one at a time. Each model should be placed Wholly Within 3" of the Leading Model and able to trace a Coherency Link to it through a continuous chain of imaginary lines drawn model-to-model. between models from the same Unit. Coherency Links cannot pass through enemy models orfrom other Units, terrain or gaps that the Leading Model cannot move through. (see Section 1.6.6). Any model unable to find a position that maintains the Coherency Link is immediately removed as a casualty. A Coherency Link can be drawn through models the Unit is in Engagement with. UNIT IN COHERENCY A Unit is In Coherency if all of its models are Wholly Within 3” of its Leading Model and Coherency Links can be drawn from each other model in the Unit to the Leading Model. Models unable to fit Wholly Within 3” must be placed as close as possible to the Leading Model while still maintaining the Coherency Link. OUT OF COHERENCY If, at any time, a model from a Unit is not Wholly Within 3” of its Leading Model, the Unit is Out of Coherency.

1.6.5 DIRECTLY TOWARDS &amp; DIRECTLY AWAY Some abilities may force a modelunit to move directly towards or directly away from another modelunit on the battlefield. When this is the case, the path of this move is determined by drawing an imaginary straight line from the center of the acting model’s base through the center of the affectedtargeted model’s base. The affected model must adhere to this line when moving in a straight line directly towards or directly away from the actingtarget model. However, if the path of the imaginary line is blocked by terrain or another model, the affected model must bypass them via the shortest possible route to continue in the direction specified. If there are two valid equidistant paths, the controlling player of the affected model may choose which one the affected model will take. When moving directly towards, and the affected Unit contains more than one model, the Leading Model must be the model physically closest to the target the Unit is moving towards. Move the Leading Model first. The remaining models must then be Placed as close to the target as possible, while maintaining Unit Coherency. Models that cannot end a movement closer to the target cannot move at all. If the target Unit contains more than one model, the imaginary line must be drawn towards the center of the base of the model physically closest to the acting model. When moving directly away, and the affected Unit contains more than one model, the Leading Model must be the model physically farthest from the target the Unit is moving away from. Move the Leading Model first. The remaining models must then be Placed as far from the target as possible, while maintaining Unit Coherency. Models that cannot end a movement farther from the target cannot move at all. If the target Unit contains more than one model, the imaginary line must be drawn towards the center of the base of the model physically farthest from the acting model.

3.4 DEPLOYMENT AND RESERVES In StarCraft: The Miniatures Game, armies do not begin the battle already in formation on the battlefield. Instead, players must manage logistics in real time - bringing forces onto the field as the round unfolds and as Supply allows. At the start of each game, no Units are deployed on the battlefield. All Units begin in Reserves. 3.4.1 THE SUPPLY POOL Deployment is governed by the Supply Pool. This represents the logistical capacity available to support troops on the frontline. At the start of each Game Round, the player’s Supply Pool is set to the value defined by the Mission Card: Round 1: Equal to the Mission's Starting Supply. Later Rounds: Increases by the Supply Escalation value each round. Hard Cap: The Supply Pool acts as a hard limit. At no point in the game can the Total Current Supply of your Units on the battlefield exceed your Supply Pool. 3.4.2 CALCULATING AVAILABLE SUPPLY During the Movement Phase, players may order Units from Reserves to enter the battlefield. To do so, they must check their Available Supply: Available Supply = (Current Supply Pool) minus (Total Supply of Friendly Units currently on the Battlefield). Deployment Cost: A Unit may onlycan be deployedfielded on the battlefield only if its Current Supply Value is less than or equal to the player's Available Supply. As Units on the battlefield are destroyed (or their Supply drops due to casualties), they free up your Available Supply, allowing new reinforcements to arrive in later rounds.

4.3 PHASE 1: THE MOVEMENT PHASE During the Movement Phase, Units on the battlefield reposition and Reserves enter the fray. Before any actions are taken, follow these steps: RESOLVE START OF THE ROUND EFFECTS Resolve any rules, abilities, or Tactical Cards that trigger at the "Start of the Round". If both players have effects, the holder of the First Player Marker resolves theirs first. CHECK SUPPLY All players must verify the current Available Supply. Activate a Unit without an Activation Marker in Reserves: Perform a Move action to enter the battlefield. Once a Unit finishes its action, place an Activation Marker (Movement Side) next to it. 4.3.2 PASSING The first player to Pass claims the First Player Marker for the next Phase. Once a player Passes, they cannot Activate any more Units in the current Phase. Mark Remaining Units Immediately place an Activation Marker (Movement Side) next to each of your Units on the battlefield that did not Activate. These Units count as having performed a Hold action for the remainder of the phase.

4.7 PHASE 3: COMBAT PHASE During the Combat Phase, Engaged Units move to close the distance to their foes and trade blows in brutal melee combat. Unlike the previous phases where activation is voluntary, all Units currently in Engagement Range must act in this Phase. You cannot choose to skip a fight. ACTIVATIONS Players take turns activating one Engaged Unit at a time, starting with the player chosen by the holder of the First Player Marker. When an eligible Unit is Activated, it follows the Close Combat SequenceAction below. UNIT ELIGIBILITY Only Engaged Units are allowed to act in this Phase. Once a Unit’s combat is resolved, remove its Activation Marker. PASSING A player only Passes when they have no Engaged Units remaining to Activate. When both players Pass, the phase ends. 4.7.1 THE CLOSE COMBAT SEQUENCEACTION CLOSE RANKS This step is optional. Before rolling any dice, the Unit acts to surround the enemy and bring more weapons to bear: The controlling player selects a Leading Model and moves it up to 3". This move follows Movement rules (Section 4.4.2) but has two specific restrictions: The Leading Model may only move if it ends the move closer to the Unit(s) it is Engaged with. This move cannot cause the Unit to become Engaged with any new enemy Units that were not already Engaged at the start of this Phase. Reposition the rest of the Unit into Coherency with the Leading Model. Models must be placed in Base-to-Base contact with enemy models whenever possible. If Base-to-Base contact is not possible, place as many models as possible in Base-to-Base contact with friendly models from their own Unit that are within Engagement Range of the enemy. Models that are already in Base-to-Base contact at the start of this step are not moved. They are physically pinned by the enemy and cannot be placed any closer. Models from the Unit making this move cannot Disengage from other enemy units.

4.8 PHASE 4: THE SCORING &amp; CLEANUP PHASE During the Scoring &amp; Cleanup Phase, the players determine which Units control Mission Markers, if any special Mission Objectives have been completed and refresh the battlefield for the next Game Round. The player holding the First Player Marker initiates the steps in the following order: Determine Mission Marker Control Score Victory Points End of Game Check Resolve END OF THE ROUND Rules Cleanup &amp; Refresh Determine Initiative 4.8.1 DETERMINE MISSION MARKER CONTROL In StarCraft, Control is determined by Supply, not model count. Follow these steps: IDENTIFY CONTESTING UNITS A Unit is eligible to Contest a Mission Marker only if it meets all three of the following criteria: It is currently on the battlefield (not in Reserves). At least one of its models is Within 3" and LoS of the Mission Marker. This model must be on the same elevation level as the Mission Marker. The Unit is In Coherency (Abators’ Call: Remember, Units that are Out of Coherency cannot control Mission Markers). CALCULATE CONTROL For each Mission Marker: Each player calculates the Total Current Supply Value of all their eligible contesting Units. The player with the higher Supply Value Controls the marker. If both player’s Total Current Supply Value is tied, the marker is Contested. A Unit reduced to Supply 0 can still Control a marker if there are no enemy models contesting it. STICKY CONTROL If a player Controls a marker, they place a Faction Indicator on the marker to signify control. Once controlled, a marker remains under that player’s Control (even if they move all their Units away) until it is explicitly reclaimed by the opponent. If a marker becomes Contested (tied score), Control does not change: If previously controlled, it remains with the current owner. If previously uncontrolled, it remains neutral. 4.8.2 SCORING VICTORY POINTS (VPs) Both players simultaneously tally their VPs for the round. Consult the Mission Card and Mission Rules for specific scoring conditions (e.g. "Gain 2 VPs for each Controlled Marker associated with the Opponent’s colour."). 4.8.3 END OF GAME CHECK Before refreshing the battlefield, check if the game ends immediately. The game ends if: The Special Winning Condition has been fulfilled, the game ends immediately and the player who met the criteria automatically wins the game. A player has no models remaining on the battlefield orand in Reserves. If the game ends due to Total Elimination, the surviving player gains +10 VP. The final Phase of the final Game Round (usually Round 5) has been completed (check 4.9 The Final Score). If the game ends, proceed directly to Section 4.8.1 Victory Conditions. Otherwise, proceed to Resolve END OF ROUND Effects. 4.8.4 RESOLVE END OF ROUND EFFECTS Resolve any rules, abilities, or Tactical Cards that trigger "At the End of the Round". If both players have effects, the holder of the First Player Marker resolves theirs first. 4.8.5 CLEANUP &amp; REFRESH Remove all Tokens and Markers from the battlefield: Do not remove Tokens with the STAY IN PLAY keyword. Do not remove Damage Markers. Do not remove Mission Markers Do not remove Faction Indicators on Mission Markers. Flip all Exhausted Tactical and Faction Cards back to their active (face-up) side. They are now ready to generate resources (CP, BM, PE) for the next round. 4.8.6 DETERMINE INITIATIVE Check the current total Victory Points. The First Player Marker is awarded to the player with fewer Victory Points than their opponent. If the score is tied, perform a Roll-Off. The winner takes the First Player Marker. Proceed to Phase 1: The Movement Phase of the next Game Round.

A Special Ability is always written using a consistent structure to ensure clarity: Name: The ability’s official designation. 5.2 TYPES OF ABILITIES ACTIVE ABILITIES Active Abilities represent manoeuvres, technologies (like Stimpacks), or enhancements that require a deliberate command to execute. An Active Ability iscan generallyonly be used while a friendly Unit is currently Activated. It may be triggered immediately before declaring an Action, or immediately after an Action has been fully resolved. It cannot be used during an Action. If the ability is on a Unit, it cannot be used while that Unit is in Reserves. The Unit must be deployed on the battlefield to use it. Unless stated otherwise, modifiers and effects granted by an Active Ability expire at the End of the Current Round. LIMITATIONS Once Per Round: A specific Unit may use a named Active Ability only once per Game Round,unless the ability has the REPETITIVE Keyword. This limit applies individually to each Unit. Two different Marine squads may both use Stimpack in the same round, but a single squad cannot use it twice. If a Unit gains access to an Active Ability via a Tactical Card, that Unit is still bound by this limit, regardless of how many copies of the Tactical Card the player controls. 5.2.1 PASSIVE ABILITIES Passive Abilities represent innate traits, permanent equipment, or constant biological adaptations: Passive Abilities are always active as long as the Unit is on the battlefield. They do not require the Unit to be Activated to function. Like Active abilities, Passive abilities on a Unit are not in effect while that Unit is in Reserves, unless explicitly stated otherwise. 5.2.2 REACTION ABILITIES Reaction Abilities allow a player to act outside the normal sequence, responding to specific events: Trigger: Used in direct response to a defined event (e.g. "When this Unit is targeted by an attack"). A Reaction must be declared at the exact moment of the trigger. If the window is missed, the ability cannot be used. A Reaction cannot be used while the Unit is in Reserves unless stated otherwise. A player may resolve only one Reaction per each Activation. EXAMPLE If a Zealot Unit is attacked, it may use Zealous Round OR Ground Armor, but not both in the same enemy attack action. If both players wish to use a Reaction to the same trigger: The Active Player (whose turn it currently is) resolves their Reaction first. The Opponent resolves their Reaction second. NO INTERRUPTION A player cannot use any Special Ability while an Action is currently in progress (e.g. between a Hit roll and an Armour roll, or while a model is physically moving across the battlefield), unless the ability is explicitly a Reaction designed to interrupt that specific sequence. LIMITATIONS: Reaction Limit: A player may resolve only one Reaction per each Activation. Once Per Round: A specific Unit may use a named Reaction Ability only once per Game Round. This limit applies individually to each Unit. Two different Medic squads may both use Life Support in the same round, but a single squad cannot use it twice. If a Unit gains access to a Reaction Ability via a Tactical Card, that Unit is still bound by this limit, regardless of how many copies of the Tactical Card the player controls. No Interruption: A player cannot use any Special Ability while an Action is currently in progress (e.g. between a Hit roll and an Armour roll, or while a model is physically moving across the battlefield), unless the ability is explicitly a Reaction designed to interrupt that specific sequence.
